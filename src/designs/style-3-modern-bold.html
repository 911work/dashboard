TL;DR

Yes, you can get a Portuguese residence permit through vocational training. Enroll in a DGERT-certified course, show €10,000 in bank proof, and apply under Article 92 (in-country) or Article 91.4 (D4 visa). Process takes 2–4 months. Tuition starts at €875/year.

FAQ

Do I need a university degree?
No. DGERT vocational courses only require a high school diploma.
Can I work while studying?
Yes. Your residence permit allows 20 hours/week of work, earning approximately €600–900/month.
Can I bring my family?
Yes, through family reunification. Your spouse also gets a work permit.
What happens after my course?
5 options: continue studying, find work, start a business, get permanent residency (5 years), or bridge to another EU country.
