How to Get a Portugal Residence Permit Through Vocational Training

A complete, step-by-step guide to obtaining legal residency in Portugal through DGERT-certified vocational courses — no university degree needed.

⚡ Quick Answer

Yes, you can get a Portuguese residence permit through vocational training. Enroll in a DGERT-certified course, show €10,000 in your bank account, and apply under Article 92 (inside Portugal) or Article 91.4 (D4 visa from abroad). The process takes 2–4 months. Costs start at €875/year for tuition.

Legal Framework: Articles 91.4 & 92

Portugal offers two pathways for international students to obtain residence permits through vocational education. Understanding which applies to your situation is the first critical step.

Feature Article 92 (In-Country) Article 91.4 (D4 Visa)
Apply from Inside Portugal Your home country
Processing time 60–90 days 30–60 days
Cost (AIMA fees) €545 €108
Best for Already in Portugal (tourist visa) Applying from India, Nigeria, etc.

What is DGERT?

DGERT (Direção-Geral do Emprego e das Relações de Trabalho) is Portugal's official body that certifies vocational training schools. Only courses from DGERT-certified institutions are valid for residence permit applications.

⚠️ Important Warning

Not all language schools or training centers qualify. Before enrolling, verify:

  • School appears in the DGERT official database
  • Your specific course is DGERT-certified
  • Certificate specifies "formação profissional"
  • School provides enrollment letter for AIMA

Step-by-Step Process

1

Choose a DGERT-Certified Course

Browse courses in data science, digital marketing, UX/UI design, or project management.

2

Get Your Enrollment Letter

School issues official acceptance confirming course dates, hours, and DGERT certification.

3

Prepare Financial Proof

Show €10,000+ in your bank account for the past 3 months.

4

Gather Required Documents

Passport, criminal record, health insurance, proof of accommodation.

5

Submit Application

Apply at AIMA (in Portugal) or Portuguese consulate (from abroad).

6

Attend Biometrics Appointment

Provide fingerprints and photo for your residence card.

7

Receive Your Residence Card

Valid for 1 year, renewable as long as you're enrolled in DGERT courses.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a university degree?
No. DGERT vocational courses only require a high school diploma. This is one of the main advantages over traditional university pathways.
How much does the entire process cost?
Budget €12,500–€18,000 for the first year. This includes tuition (€875–5,000), bank proof (€10,000), insurance (€300–600), AIMA fees, and initial accommodation.
Can I work while studying?
Yes. Your residence permit allows you to work up to 20 hours per week during term time, earning approximately €600–900 per month.
Can I bring my family?
Yes, through family reunification after you receive your residence card. Your spouse can also get a work permit.
