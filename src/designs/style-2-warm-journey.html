The Short Answer

Yes! Enroll in a DGERT-certified course → show €10,000 bank proof → apply under Article 92 or 91.4 → get your residence card in 2–4 months. No university degree required. Tuition starts at just €875/year.

Two Pathways to Your Permit

Portugal offers two legal routes depending on where you're applying from. Choose the one that fits your situation.

🏠

Article 92 — In Portugal

Apply from inside Portugal if you're already here on a tourist visa. Processing: 60–90 days. AIMA fee: €545.

✈️

Article 91.4 — D4 Visa

Apply from your home country at the Portuguese consulate. Processing: 30–60 days. Fee: €108.

📋

DGERT Certification

Only courses certified by DGERT qualify for residence permits. Always verify your school first.

💼

Common Questions

Do I need a university degree?
No. DGERT vocational courses only require a high school diploma. This is one of the key advantages over traditional university routes.
Can I work while studying?
Yes! Your residence permit allows 20 hours/week of work, earning approximately €600–900 monthly.
Can I bring my family?
Yes, through family reunification once you receive your residence card. Your spouse also gets a work permit.
What happens after my course?
5 options: continue studying, find employment, start a business, apply for permanent residency (after 5 years), or bridge to another EU country.
